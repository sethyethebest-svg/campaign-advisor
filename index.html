Where it gets used

The use case is simple: weather changes when people buy drinks.

Each morning, a regional operator can decide whether to protect margin, push delivery, or spend harder on acquisition.

Rain day

Shift spend from walk-in offers to delivery acquisition.

When rain weakens walk-in intent, the plan can move budget toward delivery-first acquisition and retargeting.

Hot day

Use lighter incentives when demand is already high.

On warm days, the product can recommend margin-aware offers instead of over-discounting natural demand.

Store reality

Show two options so teams can account for staffing and inventory.

The system ranks the best two choices, then lets the operator apply local judgment before launch.

Why it is credible

The logic is simple enough to explain and specific enough to operate.

The demo uses transparent sample coefficients. In production, the same interface would learn from store-day campaign history, ad spend, POS orders, and local weather.

Data pipeline

  1. Weather forecast Browser location plus Open-Meteo 7-day forecast.
  2. Business outcomes Store-day P-ROAS, CAC, orders, and campaign spend.
  3. Transparent model Additive regression with continuous temp, rain, and wind effects.
  4. Operator decision Top 2 recommendation, metric toggle, and human override.

Explainability

Campaign and weather effects stay visible

One additive model scores every day x campaign pair before ranking the top 2.

Scoring model Y_metric(day, campaign) = beta_0 + beta_campaign + beta_temp * (temp_F - 68°F) + beta_rain * rain% + beta_wind * wind_mph
